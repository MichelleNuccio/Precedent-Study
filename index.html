CALCULATING EMPIRES

A Genealogy of Power and Technology, 1500–2025

Kate Crawford and Vladan Joler (2023)

ABOUT:

Interactive research visualization, large-scale printed map, exhibition, digital platform

INTENDED AUDIENCE:

Researchers, designers, artists, architects, students, policymakers, and the general public interested in technology, AI, colonialism, and systems of power.

PROJECT OVERVIEW:

Calculating Empires is a visual genealogy that traces the relationships between technology, computation, colonialism, governance, labor, communication, and power from 1500 to the present. Through an interactive website and a large-scale printed diagram, the project proposes a historical framework for understanding contemporary artificial intelligence and computational infrastructures.
